# Glossary

## Core Terms

**Action potential duration**  
The time a cardiac tissue region remains electrically active before repolarizing. Regional differences help explain T wave behavior.

**Active / refractory tissue**
Tissue that has already depolarized and is not ready to activate again. In the app, it may still shape the field but is no longer the moving depolarization wavefront.

**Activation**  
The process of cardiac tissue depolarizing over time. In the app, activation is represented as deterministic state derived from scenario timing and cardiac-cycle time.

**Augmented limb leads**  
Leads aVR, aVL, and aVF. Each compares one limb electrode to the average of the other two limb electrodes.

**Body-surface potential**  
The electrical potential at an electrode location on the torso, created by the heart's changing electrical source distribution.

**Depolarization**  
The electrical transition that activates cardiac muscle cells. A moving depolarization wavefront creates changing electrical sources.

**Dipole**  
A simplified electrical source with a position, direction, and strength. The app may use regional dipoles to approximate active myocardial tissue.

**ECG / EKG**  
Electrocardiogram. A recording of voltage measurements derived from body-surface electrodes over time. The project uses "ECG" and "EKG" interchangeably in educational text.

**Electrode**  
A physical measurement point on the body surface, such as RA, LA, LL, or V1.

**Lead**  
A defined voltage measurement, not a physical wire path. For example, Lead I is LA minus RA.

**Lead axis**  
The approximate direction of a lead's positive measurement orientation. Useful for vector explanations, especially limb leads.

**Lead-region relationship**
An educational label for whether a selected region is best seen by a lead, viewed from the opposite side, or only indirectly related in the simplified model. It is not a clinical localization claim.

**Limb leads**  
Leads I, II, and III. They are computed from RA, LA, and LL electrode potentials.

**Myocardium**  
Cardiac muscle tissue.

**P wave**  
The ECG deflection associated primarily with atrial depolarization.

**Precordial leads**  
Chest leads V1 through V6. Each compares a chest electrode to the Wilson central terminal.

**QRS complex**  
The ECG deflection associated primarily with ventricular depolarization.

**Repolarization**  
The electrical recovery process after depolarization. Ventricular repolarization produces the T wave in the normal teaching model.

**Recovered tissue**
Tissue that has completed repolarization in the current teaching cycle and contributes little changing signal until the next activation.

**Scenario**  
A deterministic set of timing, anatomy, activation, repolarization, and display settings used to generate one educational example.

**ST segment**  
The interval after ventricular depolarization and before most ventricular repolarization. In a simplified normal model, there is little net source change.

**Synthetic teaching signal**  
A generated ECG-like waveform created for explanation. It may look plausible but is not diagnostic.

**T wave**  
The ECG deflection associated primarily with ventricular repolarization.

**Vector model**  
A first-level explanation that projects a net cardiac electrical vector onto lead axes.

**Wilson central terminal**  
The average of RA, LA, and LL electrode potentials. It is used as the reference for V1 through V6 in the simplified lead model.

## Preferred Language

Use:

- "lead measures a potential difference"
- "activation propagates through myocardium"
- "source distribution changes over time"
- "synthetic teaching signal"
- "simplified educational model"

Avoid:

- "voltage travels down the lead"
- "the ECG wire sees the wave"
- "diagnoses"
- "predicts patient condition"
- "clinically accurate simulation"
